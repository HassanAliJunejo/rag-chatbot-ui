Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

Module I — The Robotic Nervous System (ROS 2)

A comprehensive exploration of the convergence between artificial intelligence and mechanical engineering, delving into the principles that govern autonomous humanoid systems.

Chapters

Chapter I — ROS 2 Fundamentals

An in-depth examination of the Robot Operating System's second iteration, exploring its distributed architecture and communication patterns that form the backbone of modern robotics applications.

Chapter II — Robot Communication

A study of the complex communication systems that enable coordinated behavior in robotic systems, including message passing, service interactions, and action-based protocols for multi-agent coordination.

Chapter III — Sensors & Actuators

An analysis of the sensory and motor systems that allow robots to interact with their environment, covering everything from tactile feedback to precision control mechanisms for dexterous manipulation.
